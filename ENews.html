Project Business Statistics: E-news Express¶

Define Problem Statement and Objectives¶

Business Context¶

The advent of e-news, or electronic news, portals has offered us a great opportunity to quickly get updates on the day-to-day events occurring globally. The information on these portals is retrieved electronically from online databases, processed using a variety of software, and then transmitted to the users. There are multiple advantages of transmitting new electronically, like faster access to the content and the ability to utilize different technologies such as audio, graphics, video, and other interactive elements that are either not being used or aren’t common yet in traditional newspapers.

E-news Express, an online news portal, aims to expand its business by acquiring new subscribers. With every visitor to the website taking certain actions based on their interest, the company plans to analyze these actions to understand user interests and determine how to drive better engagement. The executives at E-news Express are of the opinion that there has been a decline in new monthly subscribers compared to the past year because the current webpage is not designed well enough in terms of the outline & recommended content to keep customers engaged long enough to make a decision to subscribe.

[Companies often analyze user responses to two variants of a product to decide which of the two variants is more effective. This experimental technique, known as A/B testing, is used to determine whether a new feature attracts users based on a chosen metric.]

Objective¶

The design team of the company has researched and created a new landing page that has a new outline & more relevant content shown compared to the old page. In order to test the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers, the Data Science team conducted an experiment by randomly selecting 100 users and dividing them equally into two groups. The existing landing page was served to the first group (control group) and the new landing page to the second group (treatment group). Data regarding the interaction of users in both groups with the two versions of the landing page was collected. Being a data scientist in E-news Express, you have been asked to explore the data and perform a statistical analysis (at a significance level of 5%) to determine the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers for the news portal by answering the following questions:

  1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page?

  2. Is the conversion rate (the proportion of users who visit the landing page and get converted) for the new page greater than the conversion rate for the old page?

  3. Does the converted status depend on the preferred language? [Hint: Create a contingency table using the pandas.crosstab() function]

  4. Is the time spent on the new page the same for the different language users?

Data Dictionary¶

The data contains information regarding the interaction of users in both groups with the two versions of the landing page.

  1. user_id - Unique user ID of the person visiting the website

  2. group - Whether the user belongs to the first group (control) or the second group (treatment)

  3. landing_page - Whether the landing page is new or old

  4. time_spent_on_the_page - Time (in minutes) spent by the user on the landing page

  5. converted - Whether the user gets converted to a subscriber of the news portal or not

  6. language_preferred - Language chosen by the user to view the landing page

1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than the existing landing page?¶

Perform Visual Analysis¶

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

Let:

  • Let $\mu_{\text{new}}$ be the true mean time spent on the new landing page

and

  • $\mu_{\text{old}}$ be the true mean time spent on the old landing page.

$H_0$: Users spend the same amount of time on the new landing page as on the existing landing page.

$H_0$: $\mu_{\text{new}} = \mu_{\text{old}}$

$H_a$: Users spend more time on the new landing page than on the existing landing page.

$H_a$: $\mu_{\text{new}} > \mu_{\text{old}}$

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

Based on the problem:

  1. We are comparing the means of a numerical variable (time_spent_on_the_page).
  2. We have two independent groups (users who saw the 'new' page and users who saw the 'old' page).
  3. The data is assumed to be numeric and likely continuous.
  4. Population standard deviations are unknown.
  5. One-tailed hypothesis (checking if new > old).

Given these conditions, an independent samples t-test (also known as a two-sample t-test) is the appropriate statistical test to compare the means of two independent groups.

The appropriate test is an Independent Samples t-test (Two-Sample t-test).

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

Based on the problem statement, the significance level is already provided.

The significance level ($\alpha$) is given as 5%. $\alpha = 0.05$

This means that I am willing to accept a 5% chance of incorrectly rejecting the null hypothesis when it is true.

Step 4: Collect and prepare data¶

# print the std

print('The sample standard deviation of the time spent on the old page is:', round(time_spent_old.std(),2))

The sample standard deviation of the time spent on the new page is: 1.82
The sample standard deviation of the time spent on the old page is: 2.58

The sample standard deviations of the new and old landing pages are different, suggesting that I cannot assume equal population variances when performing the t-test. I will use Welch's t-test (independent samples t-test without equal variance assumption).

There is sufficient statistical evidence, at the 5% significance level, to conclude that the mean time users spend on the new landing page is significantly greater than the mean time users spend on the existing landing page.

Since we are examining the relationship between two categorical variables ('converted' and 'language_preferred'), the appropriate statistical test is the Chi-Squared test for independence.

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

As stated in the problem description, the significance level ($\alpha$) is 5%.

$\alpha = 0.05$

Step 1: Define the null and alternate hypotheses¶

$H_0$: The mean time spent on the new landing page is the same for users of all preferred languages

($\mu_{\text{English, new}} = \mu_{\text{French, new}} = \mu_{\text{Spanish, new}}$).

$H_a$: The mean time spent on the new landing page is not the same for all preferred languages (at least one mean is different).

Step 2: Select Appropriate test¶

Since we are comparing the means of a numerical variable across three independent groups, the appropriate statistical test is the one-way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA).

Step 3: Decide the significance level¶

As stated in the problem description, the significance level ($\alpha$) is 5%.

$\alpha = 0.05$

Step 5: Check ANOVA Assumptions¶

The assumptions for a One-Way ANOVA test are:

  1. The responses for each factor level (language groups) have a normal population distribution.
  2. These distributions have the same variance (homogeneity of variances).
  3. The data are independent. Which is true from our dataset.

We can check the first two assumptions staistically by using Shapiro-Wiki test and Levene test respectively.

Conclusion and Business Recommendations¶

Based on the statistical analysis performed, here are the key findings and business recommendations for E-news Express:

Summary of Findings:

  1. Time Spent on Landing Page: The analysis showed statistically significant evidence that users spend more time on the new landing page compared to the existing landing page.
  2. Conversion Rate: The analysis found statistically significant evidence that the conversion rate (proportion of users who subscribe) for the new landing page is greater than the conversion rate for the old landing page.
  3. Conversion Status vs. Preferred Language: The analysis indicated statistically significant evidence that conversion status is dependent on the preferred language. This means that the likelihood of a user converting to a subscriber is different across the English, French, and Spanish language groups.
  4. Time Spent (New Page) vs. Language: The analysis showed statistically significant evidence that the mean time spent on the new landing page is NOT the same for users of different preferred languages. Some language groups on the new page spend, on average, different amounts of time than others.

Business Recommendations:

  1. Fully Implement the New Landing Page: Based on the statistically significant positive results for both time spent and conversion rate, the new landing page is superior to the old one in terms of user engagement and driving subscriptions. E-news Express should proceed with fully replacing the old landing page with the new design for all users.
  2. Leverage Language-Specific Insights: The finding that conversion and time spent on the new page are related to preferred language is crucial.
  • Identify High-Performing Languages: Determine which specific language(s) show the highest conversion rates and time spent on the new page and make improvements.
  • Targeted Content & Design: Invest in tailoring content, offers, or even slight variations in the design of the new page specifically for language groups that show higher engagement and conversion. For language groups with lower performance, investigate potential reasons (e.g., translation quality, cultural relevance of content, user preferences) and make improvements.
  • Language-Specific Campaigns: Consider launching targeted marketing or re-engagement campaigns based on user language preference, highlighting features or content most relevant to those groups.
  1. Continue Monitoring and Iterating: A/B testing is a powerful tool for continuous improvement.
  • Monitor Key Metrics: After full implementation, continue to monitor time spent, conversion rates, and other relevant metrics (e.g., bounce rate) for the new page overall and segmented by language.

  • Explore User Behavior: Dig deeper into user behavior data (e.g., click patterns, scroll depth) on the new page, potentially segmented by language, to understand why users are spending more time and converting, and what might be hindering conversion for other groups.

In conclusion, this experiment provides strong evidence to adopt the new landing page.
